Differences Between Moroccan and Kilim Rugs

Kilim rugs, on the other hand, have a distinct flatweave style that emphasizes geometric patterns and bright colors. These rugs are usually crafted from a blend of wool and cotton, offering a lightweight alternative to the thicker, plush feel of Moroccan designs. The weaving process for kilims involves a unique technique that produces a durable yet flexible fabric, perfect for various uses beyond just floor coverings. Shoppers looking for the Best Custom Rug Store for Natural Wool Berber Rugs should consider whether they prefer the rich textures and stories of Moroccan rugs or the playful patterns and practicality of kilims.

  • The patterns in Moroccan rugs often reflect tribal symbolism and storytelling.
  • Kilim designs are typically more geometric and abstract, focusing on visual harmony.
